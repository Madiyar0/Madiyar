Back and Biceps exercises

Let's start pumping our chest and triceps!

Traction of the upper block to the chest

Sit on a bench, press your feet to the floor, grasp the handle with a straight grip slightly wider than the shoulders — this position of the hands will allow you to load the widest muscles more.

Traction of the lower block to the stomach

In addition to the widest ones, the stomach pull loads the middle part of the trapezium and the rhomboid muscles well. Therefore, if you want to pump both the upper and lower back with one exercise.

Pull-ups with a straight grip

For maximum loading of the widest muscles, use a straight grip slightly wider than the shoulders. If possible, try rotating pads on the horizontal bar: they not only pump the hands and forearms, but also load the back more.

Lifting the barbell on the biceps

Basic biceps exercise. When lifting and lowering the weight, the movement is as close as possible to the natural movement of the elbow and shoulder joint. When lifting the barbell on the biceps, make sure that the body does not sway, and the elbows are fixed at one point and the elbows.

Lifting the EZ-barbell on the biceps

The use of a curved barbell increases the involvement of the biceps in work, since with a slight turn of the palm, its inner part is more actively involved in movement. The result of performing such a variation of the exercise is a more voluminous and "spherical" biceps.

Lifting dumbbells "hammer"

The more you turn your arm around during the exercise, the more noticeably the short part of the biceps is included in the work. However, make sure that the body does not sway, but only the forearm moves — the elbow should be fixed, but not pressed tightly to the body.
